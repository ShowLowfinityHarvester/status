NOAA Weather Radio Streams

KEC94 - NOAA Weather Radio
Online

Transmitter Type: VHF Cell Wireless Transmitter

Location: Phoenix, AZ

Streamed With: RTL-SDR V4 (Installed: January 7, 2024)

KEC94 broadcasts to the Phoenix, Arizona area on 162.550 MHz. Operated by NWS Phoenix, it’s located on South Mountain. Upgraded to a VHF cell wireless transmitter on February 25, 2025, it features a characteristic bit-crushed sound with a faint 60Hz background noise. Travelers’ forecasts are broadcast every 15 minutes from the hour’s start. This stream originates from our Arizona studio using an RTL-SDR V4 (acquired 2024) and a KB9VBR antenna.

WWG41 - NOAA Weather Radio
Online

Transmitter Type: VHF Cell Wireless Transmitter

Location: Payson, AZ

Streamed With: RTL-SDR V4 (Installed: January 7, 2024)

WWG41 covers the Payson, Arizona region on 162.425 MHz, operated by NWS Flagstaff from Mount Ord. It switched to a VHF cell wireless transmitter in April 2023, replacing a notoriously poor codec. Expect bit-crushed audio and a subtle 60Hz hum, louder during quiet moments—typical for this tech. No hourly observations for Payson or climate summaries are broadcast, so we recommend to check weather.gov for all of that info. WWG41 is streamed from our Arizona studio with an RTL-SDR V4 (2024) and KB9VBR antenna.

WWG42 - NOAA Weather Radio
Degraded

Transmitter Type: UHF Wireless Transmitter

Location: Globe, AZ

Streamed With: RTL-SDR V4 (Installed: January 7, 2024)

WWG42 serves the Globe, Arizona region on 162.500 MHz, operated by NWS Phoenix from Signal Peak. It broadcasts on a UHF wireless transmitter, installed in the 90s, it delivers very poor audio with heavy background static. Coverage is spotty, and it lacks hourly observations for the globe area, climate summaries, and the PSR travelers’ forecast. Visit weather.gov for all of that info. WWG42 is streamed from our Arizona studio with an RTL-SDR V4 (2024) and KB9VBR antenna.

WXL87 - NOAA Weather Radio
Online

Transmitter Type: VHF Cell Wireless Transmitter

Location: Yuma, AZ / Imperial Valley, CA

Streamed With: RTL-SDR V4 (Installed: April 27, 2024)

WXL87 broadcasts to Yuma, Arizona, and Imperial Valley, California, on 162.550 MHz. Operated by NWS Phoenix from Black Mountain, CA, its VHF cell wireless transmitter (installed June 2023) has limited range for the area it serves but offers bit-crushed audio and a 60Hz hum. Travelers forecasts play every 15 minutes from the hour. Streamed from our California studio with an RTL-SDR V4 (2024) and KB9VBR antenna, signal quality varies due to environmental factors like wind or lightning. Transmitter range and signal interference is a major problem here but we are working the best we can to resolve this issue.
